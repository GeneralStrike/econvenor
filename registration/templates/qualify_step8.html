eConvenor promises to:

  •   act ethically in our work building and maintaining eConvenor
  •   use your data only for the purpose for which we collected it unless we gain your express permission
  •   collect data only where it assists us to make eConvenor better and more accessible, or allows us to facilitate your access to eConvenor
  •   resist all and any demands for information from governments and other third parties up until the point where we are legally required to comply with a lawful demand from a democratic jurisdiction and
  •   tell you if the terms and conditions change the next time you log in

eConvenor will try as hard as possible to:

  •   keep your data secure
  •   make eConvenor accessible
  •   resist all and any demands for information from governments and other third parties
  •   ensure you can access your groups meeting, tasks, and participants data at any time (unless you break the rules) and
  •   ensure you can access all of your data in a reasonable timeframe on request (unless you break the rules).

eConvenor will not:

  •   give your data to third persons unless compelled by law
  •   sell or trade your contact details or other information or
  •   ask you for unnecessary private or personal information.

If you raise a concern with eConvenor we will:

  •   aim to respond in reasonable timeframe (considering we are a tiny volunteer organisation)
  •   examine our own conduct in good faith and with an openness to change
  •   aim to be fair in determining issues between users and
  •   apply our ethics in resolving the issue, including a will to abolish oppression, recognition of responsibility for misuse of privilege, and social equity and justice.
