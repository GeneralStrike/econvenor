Does your group fit into one or more of these categories?

  •   It operates to make a profit
  •   It is a government agency, authority or department
  •   It aims to spread or impose religion

If you group aims to spread religion you should not use eConvenor, however we see a difference between religious motivation and religious purpose. If your group is motivated by their religion to make the world better without spreading your religion or operating religious services, you can use eConvenor. For example, a christian group that organises to advocate for refugee reform, a feminist muslim women's group, a jewish anti-war group or a buddhist vegetarian food-coop may use eConvenor, as long as promoting religion isn't the aim.
